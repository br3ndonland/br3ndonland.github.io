I got rigorous research training, working in both nutritional biochemistry and engineering labs, and leading projects on a large interdisciplinary team. My tuition and living expenses were fully funded by research grants and fellowships.

I began to see the computing challenges involved in scientific research. I wrote VBA macros to automate experimental data analysis and used SAS for statistical programming _(I later switched from SAS to R -- see the [R guide page](/projects/r-guide) for more)_. I worked with Adobe Illustrator to design science illustrations.

## Classes

My coursework opened my mind to the wonderful world of cells, molecules, and biochemical reactions, and to the effects of food from cell to community.

I also did some teaching. I completed a teaching certification and ranked in the top 10% of campus teachers based on student feedback.
